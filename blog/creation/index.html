Creation

15 Jul, 2023

I’ve been analyzing what I like. Why I like certain things, etc.

There’s the obvious ones: Video games and poker. That’s simple, because I like winning. Some others are more social or romantic, fulfilling basic human needs involving other humans.

There’s one that’s odd that I haven’t quite found the answer to. And that’s art. Why do I enjoy a good movie or a book? There’s a fair argument to be made that I empathize with the characters, as in put myself into their shoes. When I watch Dune, I like to imagine that I’m Paul. Mission Impossible, Ethan Hunt. Superman, Superman.

But other types of art go beyond that. Paintings. Aesthetics seem important to me, but I’m really not sure what drives that inner interest.

2023-09-15T19:47:19-0500 is the date/time. I remember writing this in the Palantir offices, long after everybody went home. It’s been exactly two months, and I kind of left this blog to die until I decided that I wanted to write some more.

That’s now. The topic that I wanted to write about was about my desire to create. I opened the blog repo, and the unfinished post is staring back at me. It’s interesting that it’s actually a topic in the same vein, and I might as well finish it.

So what drives that interest? I’ve come to the conclusion that it’s the act of creation. Why? Part of it might be ego. I remember writing a bullshit college application essay about how writing code was an act of construction, about how I felt good laying down beams in the form of data structures, etc. It obviously wasn’t real, but it was how I wanted to feel.

It’s still here. Once I finish training in Chicago and move into my place in New York, I plan on getting the beefiest computer I can and learning Blender(the 3d modelling program). I tried poetry for a bit, but found it too boring. Similarly, I tried writing short stories but found it too slow.

The common theme is that I’m wired for action. I just can’t stay focused on something unless something is literally happening. The only exception is code. My theory is that code, in some way, does provide action, because my program does something. I can build pieces, and see them run.

But even still, I’m the most motivated when I’m competing(autotrading competitions?), or when I’m coding something that will do something that I’m interested in. When I coded Luna, my discord bot for interacting with OpenAI’s GPT APIs, I enjoyed the feeling of building that system, sure, but what I was really focused on was getting to use the thing that I created.

So visuals it is. I love looking at things. Looking at aesthetic things. I don’t have time nor the patience to learn painting. So I’ll give Blender a shot, and I’m really hoping that its similarity to a video game will push me to stick with it and create things that I like.

How’s my trading job going? It’s a sin wave. Some weeks, I feel destined for this shit. Other weeks, I feel like I’m trapped. Either way, I think I’m being shaped into a better version of myself. I’ll tell you more about it next time.
